# Marketing Standards

These standards keep marketing templates consistent, reusable, and easy to move into the workspace portal later.

## Voice

Marketing copy should feel clear, helpful, and confident. It should not sound inflated, robotic, or overly casual.

Use:

- Short sentences with a clear point.
- Specific benefits and concrete next steps.
- Warm, direct phrasing.
- A single primary call to action.

Avoid:

- "Just checking in" as the main message.
- Empty urgency like "Don't miss out" without a real reason.
- Long paragraphs that hide the action.
- Unverified claims, guarantees, or stats.
- Multiple competing asks in one template.

## Placeholder Style

Use bracketed placeholders for anything that must be customized:

```text
[Client Name]
[Offer]
[Date]
[Booking Link]
[Primary CTA]
```

Do not leave vague placeholders like `[stuff]`, `[thing]`, or `[details]`.

## Review Checklist

Before marking a template `ready`:

- Confirm the audience is correct.
- Confirm the channel is correct.
- Replace or verify all placeholders.
- Check that the CTA is visible and singular.
- Remove private, client-specific, or time-sensitive details unless they are placeholders.
- Confirm the message can be reused without rewriting from scratch.

## Email Standards

- Subject line should be specific and under 60 characters when possible.
- Preview text should add context, not repeat the subject line.
- Opening line should quickly establish relevance.
- Body should stay scannable.
- CTA should appear once near the end unless the email is long enough to justify a second mention.
- Signature should be handled by the sender or email platform unless the template requires a specific signoff.

## Social Standards

- Start with the strongest idea in the first line.
- Keep paragraphs short.
- Use hashtags only when they help discovery or categorization.
- Do not overload posts with links.
- Make the post reusable by keeping event-specific details in placeholders.

## Classified Ad Standards

- Make the title searchable and specific.
- Put location, service type, and primary offer near the top.
- Include clear pricing language or a placeholder for pricing.
- Keep the body scannable for people comparing options quickly.
- Include trust signals such as experience, studio setup, lesson format, or portfolio links.
- End with a clear reply prompt.

## Campaign Standards

- Campaign briefs should connect audience, goal, offer, channels, and reusable assets.
- A campaign can contain multiple posts and emails, but each asset should still live as its own reusable template when possible.
- Campaign files should link to related email and social templates.

## Portal Fields

Future portal indexing should be able to rely on:

- `title`
- `type`
- `channel`
- `audience`
- `status`
- `owner`
- `last_reviewed`
- `tags`
- `portal_ready`

Do not rename these fields without updating the portal metadata notes.
